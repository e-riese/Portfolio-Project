Reflections: RBC
Charity Partner Rotation

Rotation 2: Exposure to the Not-For-Profit Sector and Fostering Community Engagement with the Teach for Canada - GakinaamaageGrowth and Partnerships Team

Describe your role at the charity:

I worked with Teach for Canada - Gakinaamaage for 3 months with their Growth and Partnerships team. I worked with the team to develop a broad reach business strategy to engage major donors in the United States. This entailed developing a high touch fundraising program strategy in support of indigenous education centered around personalized corporate and foundation engagement in the United States. The work mainly involved conducting grant research and composing grant proposal drafts aimed towards well aligned potential donors.

At the charity, I was surprised by...

...how casual the atmosphere was! After working with the commercial service advisor team for 6 months where we were meeting end of day deadlines every day, it was such a contrast to experience a team that had more long-term deadlines. The Growth and Partnership team was working very hard every day, but it felt like the daily work was more focused towards the overarching long-term objectives of the organization rather than pushing to meet day to day objectives. Reflecting on it, this is probably because the nature of the work was very different. It felt like a complete 180 to the pace of the work in rotation one! The organization was also much smaller, so people were closer to one another and more familiar which fostered an exceptionally warm and welcoming work environment.

What I enjoyed most about working at the charity was...

...being able to make the work my own. I felt like in rotation one the work was quite fixed. We needed to deliver standardized responses to maintain an assured level of quality for RBC customers. There was a quality component to the work, but in many ways to showcase your efforts and talents it became much more about quantity. With this rotation, the project was much more open, and I could do as much as I felt appropriate to a quality that demonstrated a more holistic range of my knowledge and capabilities. It was easier to showcase my work ethic, my problem-solving skills and my creative thinking which are all the best of what I have to offer in work environments and team settings.

My most important realization at the charity was...

...how much I didn't know about Indigenous issues in Canada. After coming to Canada in 2016 I learned a bit about Indigenous issues here, however it felt like a one-dimensional tale of tragedy. The story told to me didn't encompass the nuances of the realities of contemporary Indigenous life in Canada and failed to celebrate the triumphs uniqueness of First Nations while still acknowledging the traumatic history. Teach For Canada Gakiaamaage is led by an Anishinaabe person, and often part of the team meetings would incorporate First Nation culture, ideas, and practices. I could see in my short time with the charity that leading the organization in this manner overall enriched the team's experience and integrity towards their work. I was inspired to learn more about First Nations culture after this work experience and I really hope that they will get to continue to do the work they do for as long as possible.
