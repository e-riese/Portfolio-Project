Capital Markets, Next Great Social Innovator Challenge and
Career Launch Journey at RBC

March 2024
End of Rotation 3: Capital Markets Institutional Client Credit Management - Developing practical skills in a corporate environment.

The Next Great Social Innovator (NGSI) Challenge is the capstone learning event of the RBC Career Launch Program. This unique challenge was an opportunity to apply the skills learned from the internship to create an innovative solution.

During the 3rd rotation, I was working at...

...the Institutional Client and Credit Management Transaction team in Capital Markets. I took over some of the daily credit management tasks of the analyst and associates while they were looking for a new permanent hire. I also took on some of the projects outside of the team's day to day work which I will be working on for the next year during my contract extension.

The Next Great Social Innovator Challenge allowed me to...

...develop and practice many skills: leadership, communication, creative thinking, public speaking, presentations making, learning new software, UX/UI design, and time management - to just name a few. Our team worked really hard and saw great results because of it. It was a close debate among the judges with an almost even 50/50 split on who they thought should be the winner, but in the end we came a very close second. I was sad we didn’t end up coming first after putting so much work into it, but we made a great idea, had a great presentation to back it up and tried our best so I don’t think of it as a loss.

One word that describes my year...

..Opportunity! I felt that this whole program has been a great experience to grow and learn. I had always flourished at school, but breaking out into the working world was a whole different challenge, especially being new to Canada. This program really gave me the mentorship and opportunity I needed to make a start at a real career. I'll always look back on this experience and be grateful that it came when it did. I’m also grateful for the doors it's opened for me, as well as the skills I have learned to be able to open more doors for myself and others in the future.

What surprised me the most was...

...how much I learned throughout the program and how much I've transformed since the beginning. It's only been a year, but so much has changed in my life because of this program. I couldn't have imagined I would be where I am today if I hadn't gone through it. There were some truly challenging moments, but I’ve come through the other side with more experience and wisdom because of it.

The experience changed me in the following ways...

... feel much more confident going into the working world and navigating the corporate environment. I understand more about what people are looking for in the corporate world and how to demonstrate and communicate that I'm able to meet those expectations. When I look back and reflect I see the vast difference in myself from being a naïve fresh grad to a confident and experienced member of the work force.

I realized...

..that I have a lot to offer! It's easy to let imposter syndrome kick in and make you feel underqualified, but successfully completing all the diverse rotations has really boosted my confidence. In each one I developed good standing relationships with my managers and the teams I was working on. People appreciated my work, my ideas and what I had to offer. I now have a much better understanding of how to succeed in the working world by being a proactive colleague rather than a more rote employee.

I am excited to...

...continue my journey with RBC Capital Markets and my career in the finance world! I'm very happy that my team has offered me contract. There's still so much to learn on this team and so many projects that they need completed. I'm really looking forward to seeing where this opportunity takes me and continuing to work with all the great people I've met so far!
