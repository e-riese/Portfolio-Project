End of RBC Operations
Commercial Banking Rotation

Rotation 1: Operations for Commercial Banking - Build your communication, teamwork and customer relationship skills by supporting clients in the Commercial Service Advisor role

My greatest lesson with the operations assignment was...

...learning how to keep organized when following up on cases. For difficult cases I did have the tendency to keep leaving them for tomorrow. But if you do that every day, you end up leaving it for weeks and scrambling to get it done. It’s easy to procrastinate the tough stuff, but in the end just facing it head on by doing the next right step to get it done is always the way to go. It feels like it takes a longer time to do, but in the end, you’ll spend less time on it overall and get it done faster. And that’s true for the Commercial Service Advisor requests as well as all difficult tasks in life.

What surprised me the most was...

...how diverse the commercial clients of RBC are and learning about the inner workings of the bank. Banks are so ingrained in day-to-day life that I forget that they are also businesses in of themselves, not public institutions. Working with commercial clients every day you realize the real reason a bank is there to offer financial products and services to generate a profit, and our work was directly making that happen. My perception of what a bank is shifted from an everyday institution in society to more of the actual business model.

It was also surprising to see the breadth and depth of the businesses across Canada working with RBC. I would get requests from all sorts of industries, like entertainment companies, retail, townships, real estate developers, transport, plumbers, electricians, dentistry, law firms and even bubble tea shops. Getting a peek inside the backbone of the Canadian economy was eye opening to what’s really happening in Canada, and what is really meant when we talk about the Canadian economy.

My favourite memory was...

...having lunch with my team every day. Even though we talked on Webex before going in office, we didn’t really get to build those connections until we were able to talk face-to-face. Once we started to get more comfortable with each other, it really became something to look forward to when coming in office. They became the people who understood what I was going through at work the most since we were all in the same boat and going through the same things. It felt good to have support and people to discuss the experience of the internship with.

I honestly feel that I’ve made genuine connections and friends through this rotation, and I hope that we will get the chance to meet up again soon!
